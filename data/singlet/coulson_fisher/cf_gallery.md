# Coulson-Fisher Point Gallery: Singlet Partition

*Generated 2026-03-29 15:36 UTC | 156 species scanned*

The Coulson-Fisher (CF) point is the geometry scaling factor $\alpha$ at which the UHF solution first diverges from RHF, indicating the onset of spin-symmetry breaking. Below the CF point, UHF collapses to RHF; beyond it, UHF breaks spin symmetry and yields lower energy.

**Note:** For polyatomic molecules, uniform geometry scaling stretches all bonds simultaneously. The CF point found here is for the uniform-scaling dissociation path, which differs from bond-specific CF points typically reported in literature.
